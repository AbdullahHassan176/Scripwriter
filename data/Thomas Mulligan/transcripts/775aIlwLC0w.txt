Why do we hate the sound of our own voice? After listening to the sound of their own voice, 67% of people claim they sound worse on a recording than they do in their own head. When you hear yourself speak, your voice is being conducted through your skull bones, which actually lowers its frequency, meaning you probably perceive your voice to be deeper and richer than it actually is. When your voice is recorded and played back for you, it's being conducted through the air and is vibrating in your eard drums and earbones, making your voice sound thinner and higher pitched than what you're used to, causing most people to respond with, "Do I really sound like that?"
