The internet is freaking out, and for good reason, because this might be the biggest news of all time. A few weeks back, the presidents of China and Russia were caught on a hot mic talking about how people this century might live to see 150 thanks to advancements in biotechnology. Just a few weeks later, and this study was published in Cell, where Chinese scientists were able to successfully reverse key signs of aging in monkeys using genetically altered human stem cells. Over a 44-week trial, elderly monkeys received a bi-weekly IV infusion of a special type of stem cell called SRC that revealed a multi-system rejuvenation across 10 major physiological systems and 61 tissue types. Treated monkeys showed improved memory, healthier brain structures, stronger bones, and restored reproductive functions. And tissue analysis revealed reduced brain atrophy, inflammation, osteoporosis, fibrosis, and liquid buildup.
