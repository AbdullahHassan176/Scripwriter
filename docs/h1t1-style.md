# h1t1 — Creator Style Guide

Derived from transcripts: `2a9oll96i38`, `rvx124ljemI`, `EKd2Pi_3mSU`, `vUUAOCJ6uJQ`, `GWbgPM3FvZA`

---

## Voice & Tone
- Conversational, direct, first-person
- Sardonic but not mean-spirited — observational wit
- Never lectures or moralizes; just states what happened and what's odd about it
- Feels like texting a smart friend who just saw something wild

## Structure (every video follows this roughly)
1. **"So,"** — almost universal opener, drops the viewer into the story mid-motion
2. **Setup** — explains the viral moment or event with concrete names/numbers/details
3. **Pivot** — introduces the twist or irony using: `"And well,"` / `"The thing is,"` / `"Obviously,"`
4. **Close** — one wry observation or dark punchline; no CTA, no subscribe ask

## Sentence Style
- Very short sentences. Punchy. Natural pauses built in.
- Avoids subordinate clauses — keeps it spoken-word simple
- Uses `"I don't know why X thought Y"` construction for light sarcasm
- Grounds abstract situations with specific proper nouns (platform names, dollar amounts, locations)

## What He Never Does
- No "smash that subscribe button" or CTAs of any kind
- No moralizing or explicit lessons ("the moral of the story is...")
- No overly optimistic framing
- No hedging language ("it seems like maybe possibly...")

## Pivot Phrases (use sparingly, one per script)
| Phrase | Function |
|---|---|
| `"And well,"` | Introduces the ironic consequence |
| `"The thing is,"` | Zooms out to the bigger point |
| `"Obviously,"` | Sarcastic commentary on the obvious |
| `"I don't know why X thought Y"` | Light sarcasm toward a bad actor |

## Timing
- Typical length: 20–60 seconds spoken
- Aim for ~80–130 words in the script body
- No filler — every sentence must earn its place

## Closing Signature
Ends on an observation, not an action. Usually slightly dark or cynical.
Examples from transcripts:
- *"It's actually concerning how much misinformation we just witnessed."*
- *"So yeah, if your phone ever gets stolen, good luck getting it."*
- *"I don't know why Denny's thought this person would side with a corporation."*
