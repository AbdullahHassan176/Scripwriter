# Script: MrBeast Editor's Insider Trading on Kalshi
**Style:** h1t1 (short-form, conversational, sardonic)
**Format:** Short-form (~45 seconds)
**Topic:** Prediction market insider trading enforcement

---

## SCRIPT

So, a MrBeast video editor was just fined for insider trading — except it wasn't the stock market. It was a prediction market.

His name is Artem Kaptur. He edited MrBeast's videos, which meant he knew exactly which videos were dropping before anyone else did. So he went on Kalshi — a platform where you can bet real money on things like "will a specific video come out this week" — and placed bets he already knew he'd win.

And well, Kalshi's own surveillance system caught him. His record was described as "near-perfect" and "statistically anomalous." He's now fined over $20,000 and banned for two years.

In the same sweep, they also caught a California governor candidate betting on his own election. Five-year ban.

The thing is, insider trading is illegal in stocks. But prediction markets are so new that they're basically writing the rules in real time. Kalshi opened 200 investigations this past year alone.

So if you're thinking about betting on these platforms — just know the person you're betting against might already know the answer.

---

## STYLE NOTES (h1t1 breakdown)
- **"So,"** opener — creator's signature entry into every story
- **Concrete details up front:** full name, platform name, dollar amount, ban length
- **"And well,"** pivot — used to introduce the consequence/reveal
- **"The thing is,"** reframe — zooms out to the bigger point without being preachy
- **Dark closing observation** — no call to subscribe, no optimistic spin; ends on a wry note
- **Short punchy sentences** throughout, natural spoken cadence
- **No moralizing** — the tone is observational/sardonic, not lecturing
